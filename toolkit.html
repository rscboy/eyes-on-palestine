Tens of thousands of civilians have been killed in Israel’s systematic attacks against Palestinians in Gaza since October 2023. As the world watches in horror, Echoes of Gaza serves as a digital archive, preserving the documentation of human rights violations gathered by researchers, journalists, and digital evidence teams. This open-source record exists to ensure that evidence of war crimes - such as the deliberate pulverization of civilian infrastructure and mass civilian casualties - cannot be rewritten or minimized.

Alongside members of the Palestinian and Jewish communities globally, human rights advocates are campaigning for the enforcement of ceasefires, the protection of civilians, and international accountability. At this moment of unprecedented humanitarian catastrophe, the actions and advocacy of the international community are critical.

It is because of everyday people standing up for human rights that governments around the world have taken initial steps - from voting at the UN to adjusting trade and aid policies. There is much more to do. This toolkit provides the resources to leverage our archive for direct action.

What needs to be done?

As a historical archive, Echoes of Gaza preserves the evidence necessary for accountability. Based on this documented reality, we support the calls of international human rights organizations urging governments worldwide to:

Uphold international law

  • Publicly and unequivocally call on Israel to adhere to IHL and IHRL.
  • Push for Israel’s compliance with the ICJ orders, including measures to prevent genocide and occupation.

Support accountability

  • Back all international investigations, including by the ICC.
  • Call on the UN Security Council to impose targeted sanctions on officials responsible for war crimes.

Suspend arms transfers

  • Suspend all direct or indirect arms transfers to Israel.
  • End all military cooperation and assistance until compliance with international law is verified.

Ensure aid & protection

  • Demand immediate, sustained and unhindered delivery of humanitarian aid to Gaza.
  • Support medical evacuations and ensure safe access for humanitarian workers.

Ban settlement trade

Impose a comprehensive ban on all trade and economic activity in illegal settlements in the Occupied Palestinian Territory.

Champion lasting peace

Advocate for a political solution that ends the occupation and respects Palestinian self-determination.

Get Informed and Share Knowledge

Understand the Designations

Thanks to relentless public pressure, various governments have shifted stances - reinstating UNRWA funding and calling for ceasefires. But it is not enough. Many governments have not acknowledged the severity of the situation, stopped arms transfers, or held violators accountable to international law.

"International human rights organizations, UN experts, and legal scholars have concluded that Israel is committing acts of genocide against Palestinians in Gaza. This includes: killing civilians in large numbers, causing serious physical and mental harm, and deliberately inflicting conditions of life calculated to bring about the destruction of the population."

Under international law, all governments have a duty to prevent genocide. Governments must no longer enable impunity. Business as usual is not an option.

Advocacy Script Library

Advocacy extends beyond elected officials. Use these structured, evidence-based scripts to demand accountability in your workplace, academic institutions, and personal networks. Select a scenario below to access the corresponding script.

Call Your Official: Script

Target: Representatives, Parliament Members, Local Government

Hello, my name is [Your Name] and I'm a local constituent.

  • Enforce the Ceasefire: I am calling to demand that our government hold Israel accountable for its daily, documented violations of the current ceasefire agreement.
  • Ensure Aid: I urge [Official's Name] to demand the immediate and unhindered delivery of humanitarian aid, which continues to be obstructed.
  • End Arms Transfers: Our government must suspend all arms transfers and military cooperation until there is full compliance with international law and the ceasefire.
  • Support Accountability: Our government must back efforts at the UN and international courts to impose targeted sanctions on those responsible for ongoing war crimes.
  • Uphold Law: We must push for Israel to follow all ICJ orders to prevent genocide and protect civilians.

Corporate Accountability: Email

Target: Corporate Leadership, Human Resources, Union Reps

Subject: Request for Supply Chain Review and Human Rights Compliance

Dear [Leadership/HR Name],

I am writing to formally request that [Company Name] review its vendor and supply chain relationships to ensure we are not financially complicit in documented human rights violations.

As the crisis in Gaza has escalated into what international legal experts and the UN have identified as plausible genocide, corporations hold a responsibility under the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights to conduct strict due diligence.

I urge leadership to establish a clear, transparent policy regarding our financial investments and partnerships. I would appreciate a meeting to discuss how [Company Name] plans to address this institutional responsibility.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Academic Divestment: Email / Letter

Target: University Presidents, Board of Trustees, Deans

Subject: Call for Endowment Transparency and Divestment

Dear [Title and Name],

As a [student/alumnus/faculty member], I am formally requesting full transparency regarding the university's endowment investments, specifically concerning corporations complicit in the military occupation and ongoing attacks on civilian infrastructure in Gaza.

Academic institutions have a historic and moral duty to stand against documented war crimes. I urge the Board of Trustees to commit to a comprehensive divestment strategy.

Furthermore, I request clear, documented protections for students and faculty exercising their right to protest and document these violations on campus, free from administrative retaliation.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]

Navigating Skepticism: Talking Points

Target: Skeptical Family Members, Community Forums

Core Principles for the Conversation:

  • Redefine Success: "Acknowledge that completely changing someone's mind is often ambiguous. Getting them to understand your vantage point - and understanding theirs - is a more attainable baseline."
  • Maintain Dignity: "Keep the conversation civil and treat others with baseline decency. If they refuse to return that respect, their conduct speaks louder than their argument."
  • Anchor in Evidence: "I rely on documented evidence and international human rights consensus, not partisan media."
  • Cite the Record: "Organizations like Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and the UN have extensively documented the systemic attacks on civilian infrastructure and the obstruction of aid."
  • Reject False Neutrality: "We can disagree on political solutions, but the archival record of mass civilian casualties is verified and factual. It cannot be minimized."
  • Focus on the Law: "This isn't just about my personal feelings; the International Court of Justice has identified a plausible risk of genocide. International law requires accountability."

Deploying the Archive

When advocating online or in person, your strongest tool is verified documentation. Drop these curated, undeniable sources directly into emails, social media comments, or conversation notes to counter denialism and establish the factual record in seconds.

Live Advocacy Scenarios

The Encountered Claim

"This is a targeted operation focused strictly on combatants, utilizing precision strikes."

Strategic Guidance

Do not engage in emotional debate. Neutralize the claim by deploying the military's own internal data. Keep the tone factual, public, and undeniable.

Citation to Deploy

Revealed: Israeli military’s own data indicates civilian death rate of 83% in Gaza war. (The Guardian)

https://www.theguardian.com/world/ng-interactive/2025/aug/21/revealed-israeli-militarys-own-data-indicates-civilian-death-rate-of-83-in-gaza-war

Example Responses

Social Media Comment
You claim this is a targeted operation, but the Israeli military’s own data reveals an 83% civilian death rate. This is a documented war against non-combatants. The factual record contradicts your statement. https://www.theguardian.com/world/ng-interactive/2025/aug/21/revealed-israeli-militarys-own-data-indicates-civilian-death-rate-of-83-in-gaza-war

Dear [Name], your recent statement ignores the factual record. According to independent analysis of the Israeli military's own data, the civilian death rate is 83%. We demand you acknowledge this reality and call for an arms embargo immediately. Public Comment / Speaking Notes
When we discuss the death toll today, we are not dealing in estimates or disputed figures. The IDF itself has accepted the Health Ministry's estimate of over 70,000 Palestinians killed, as reported by Haaretz. The debate over numbers is a deliberate distraction from the reality of the slaughter. My question is: now that the occupying force has admitted the scale of this genocide, what concrete actions is this council taking to address it?

"As an institution, we must remain strictly neutral and avoid taking sides in a complex geopolitical conflict."

Strategic Guidance

Pivot from "political conflict" to "humanitarian consensus." Emphasize that neutrality in the face of systemic harm to protected groups (like children) violates basic institutional ethics.

Save the Children: Israel Has Killed on Average a Child Every Hour for Past 23 Months.

Meeting Notes / Verbal Pivot
This isn't about taking a political side; it's about basic human rights. According to Save the Children, a child has been killed every hour for the past 23 months. Our institution's commitment to ethics and human rights cannot be suspended just because applying those principles has become politically uncomfortable.
Follow-up Email Template
Following our discussion on institutional neutrality, I urge leadership to review the documented reality on the ground. When organizations like Save the Children report the unprecedented killing of youth at this scale, neutrality reads as complicity. Please see the attached documentation.

The Encountered Claim

"The growing humanitarian crisis and starvation are unfortunate byproducts of urban warfare."

Strategic Guidance

Challenge the passive voice. Starvation is not a byproduct; it is a policy. Use logistical facts regarding aid blockage to prove deliberate intent.

Citation to Deploy

Israel allows only 1/3 of aid trucks into Gaza under ceasefire deal, authorities say. (Middle East Monitor)

https://www.middleeastmonitor.com/20251124-israel-allows-only-1-3-of-aid-trucks-into-gaza-under-ceasefire-deal-authorities-say/

Example Responses

Letter to the Editor / Comment
Describing the starvation in Gaza as an 'unfortunate byproduct' ignores the factual record of deliberate obstruction. Authorities confirm that Israel is allowing only one-third of agreed-upon aid trucks to enter, even under ceasefire conditions. This is not an accident of urban warfare; it is a systematic policy of deprivation. The media must report on this intentionally engineered blockade accurately.
